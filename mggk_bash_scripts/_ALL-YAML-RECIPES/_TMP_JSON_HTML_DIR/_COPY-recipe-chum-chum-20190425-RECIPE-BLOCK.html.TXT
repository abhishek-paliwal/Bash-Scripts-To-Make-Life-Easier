            <span itemprop='description'>Chum Chum (Cham Cham/Chomchom) is a popular authentic traditional Sweet Dish which hails from the Eastern Indian state of West Bengal. That’s why this sweet dish is also known as Bengali Mithai. Chum Chum is made with Paneer (Indian cottage cheese). This Recipe post explains how to make soft and spongy Cham Cham at home.</span>

                            <h4>INSTRUCTIONS:</h4>
                            <div itemprop='recipeInstructions'><h4>How to Make Chenna:</h4><ol><li>Add full cream cow milk to a thick bottom pan and boil it.</li><li>Once it is boiling, then you add some lemon juice or vinegar to curdle it.</li><li>When you see the greenish whey separated from the solids, then your milk is curdled. (You can use this whey for making chapati dough).</li><li>Line a strainer/colander with a cheese/muslin cloth.</li><li>Once the milk curdles, pass it through the lined strainer.</li><li>After the above step, drain off the water on the muslin cloth.</li><li>Rinse it well with ice-cold water or under cold running water.</li><li>Now gather the cloth from the sides. Remove all the water by squeezing the cloth.</li><li>Now you have your chenna/paneer (coagulated milk) ready.</li><li>Chenna ­needs to be dried well.</li><li>Hang the cloth for 45 minutes to remove excess water.</li></ol><h4>How to Make Chenna (Paneer) Dough:</h4><ol><li>Once the dough is hanged for about 45 minutes, you remove the chenna out from the cloth and knead well for about 8-10 minutes or until dough forms.</li><li>Knead well until it starts leaving the fat, or you see your palms are getting greasy.</li><li>You can also use a food processor for kneading.</li></ol><h4> How to Shape Chum Chum:</h4><ol><li>Divide the dough into 8-10 equal pieces.</li><li>Take one portion between the palms of your hands and roll into a 4 inch cylindrical or oval shape.</li><li>Repeat the same to make more ovals.</li><li>Smoothen the rolls and make sure that there are no cracks. Set them aside.</li></ol><h4>Making Sugar Syrup:</h4><ol><li>Add 2 cups sugar, 5 cups water to a large pot and stir with a spatula.</li><li>Let the water boil for 8-10 minutes. (You don't need to look for any thread consistency.)</li><li>Sugar syrup is ready for cooking chum chum.</li></ol><h4>How to Cook Chum Chum:</h4><ol><li>Once the syrup has boiled, gently drop the cheese rolls into it and cover it.</li><li>Cook covered for 10 minutes on high heat.</li><li>Turn the heat to medium-low and cook for 5 minutes.</li><li>Let them cool completely at room temperature.</li></ol><h4> How to Make Mawa Stuffing:</h4><ol><li>Heat a pan over medium heat.</li><li>Add 1 tablespoons ghee and 1/4 cup crumbled mawa to it.</li><li>Sauté for 1 minute and add saffron milk (crushed saffron strands soaked in warm milk.)</li><li>Once the mawa melts, add the cream and cook for 5-7 minutes over medium-low heat.</li><li>Add sugar and mix everything well.</li><li>Once the mixture starts to thicken, add cardamom powder and mix well. Cook for 2 more minutes.</li><li>Don’t let it become dry completely, because this mixture thickens after cooling.</li><li>Let it cool completely at room temperature.</li></ol><h4> How to Assemble Chum Chum:</h4><ol><li>Once the chum chum has cooled off, remove chum chum from sugar syrup and press it lightly between your palms to take out the extra syrup.</li><li>Make a lengthwise slit halfway through, using a knife.</li><li>Fill with the cooled mawa stuffing and smoothen the top.</li><li>Roll it over desiccated coconut and top with some chopped pistachios and glazed cherries.</li><li>Serve chilled.</li></ol></div>

                            <h3>NOTES:</h3>Do not stir chum chum with a spoon while boiling, they will break.<br>You can also add some rose or kewara essence to the dough for a fragrant floral flavour.<br>I did not add any binder (flour) to the paneer because I like spongy chum chum. But some people like to add arrowroot powder, all-purpose flour (maida), cornstarch or even semolina (suji) to get a firm texture. If you also want stiff texture of Chum chum, then add 1 tablespoon of any of the above binders to the crumbled paneer before kneading it.
